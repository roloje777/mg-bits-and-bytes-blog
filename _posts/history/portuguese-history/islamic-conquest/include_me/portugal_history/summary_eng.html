  1. Roman rule (3rd century BC - 4th century AD)

    The first Roman invasion of the Iberian Peninsula occurred in 219 BC. Within 200 years, almost the entire peninsula was annexed to the Roman Republic, beginning the Romanization of Hispania.
    The Carthaginians, Rome's opponents in the Punic Wars, were expelled of its coastal colonies.

  2. Germanic kingdoms (5th to 7th centuries)

    At the beginning of the 5th century, Germanic tribes invaded the peninsula namely the Suevi, the Vandals (Silingi and Hasdingi) and their allies, the Sarmatians and the Alans.

  3. THE MOORS IN PORTUGAL (711 AD - 1294 AD)

    Large parts of Portugal were occupied by Islamic rulers from 711 until 1294 beginning with an invasion by the Northern Umayyad Caliphate Africa.
    Like the Romans before them, the Moors expanded their control over what is today Portugal from its main power base in Spain. They They came from the south and headed north and west.

  4. The French (1807 - 1811)

    5. A crucial factor in Napoleon's ambitions for the Iberian Peninsula involved the conquest and domination of Portugal. There would be a total of three French campaigns in Portugal during this period – the first in 1807-08, the second in 1809 and the third in 1810-11 – all of which resulted in a disastrous defeat.
