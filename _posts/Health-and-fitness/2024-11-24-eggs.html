The word egg tracks it name back to a prehistoric Indo-European, which was the word for bird.

Neolithic artifacts from China

Eggs have been known to, and enjoyed by, humans for many centuries.
Jungle fowl were domesticated in India by 3200 B.C.E.
Records from China and Egypt show that fowl were domesticated and laying eggs for human consumption around 1400 B.C.E., and The Romans found egg-laying hens in England, Gaul, and among the Germans.

Although eggs are a breakfast staple for many people, they tend get a bad rap for their cholesterol content.

"While past research confused the public regarding if it was truly safe to eat eggs on a regular basis out of fear of cardiovascular disease, recent research has since disproved this notion," said registered dietitian Elizabeth Shaw.
